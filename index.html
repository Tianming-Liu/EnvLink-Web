Portable multi-modal environmental sensing for underrepresented human habitats. We follow human habitats overlooked, or poorly captured by existing data platforms.
Global monitoring networks cluster around cities, and places with reliable infrastructure. Dense dashboards from these nodes never represent the totality of human–environment interaction.
Villages, mountain valleys, riverbank settlements, and informal edges rarely host instruments. Yet they hold generations of adaptation knowledge dismissed as “blank spots” These are not voids—they are archived habitats waiting to be recorded again.

Sensor architecture

EnvLink Wearable Device

Five synchronized sensing modules translate remote habitats into multi-modal evidence.

What we lose is more than data; it is the accumulated wisdom of dwelling with landscapes. We need sensing that can walk into real habitats and stay with people, not just towers. Our wearable multi-modal system fuses time, space, and physiology to bring edge communities back into the map.
